Folk rock is a subgenre of rock music that draws heavily
on English and American folk music. It emerged in the mid-1960s
when folk singers like Bob Dylan and Roger McGuinn of the Byrds
picked up electric guitars, and when rock bands like the
Animals turned to traditional folk for inspiration.
